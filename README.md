# Inner Compass — Psychedelic Journey Journal

A personal, private web app for journaling before and after psychedelic experiences. Built on validated research instruments from Johns Hopkins, Imperial College London, and other leading psychedelic research institutions.

**This is not a clinical tool, not for analytics, not for data collection.** The sole purpose is to help each individual user see the shifts in their own thought process, emotional landscape, and sense of self across the arc of a psychedelic experience.

---

## Why This Exists

Psychedelic experiences can produce profound shifts in consciousness, but the insights often fade or become difficult to articulate as time passes. Research consistently shows that **structured reflection** — before, during integration, and weeks after — is one of the strongest predictors of lasting positive change.

The problem: most validated instruments (MEQ-30, EDI, EBI, etc.) are designed for clinical trials and buried in academic papers. This app makes them accessible as a personal reflection tool, with the added ability to **compare your own before/after responses side by side**.

## Core Research Instruments Used

### 1. MEQ-30 — Mystical Experience Questionnaire (30 items)
- **Source**: Johns Hopkins University (Barrett, Johnson & Griffiths, 2015)
- **Paper**: [Validation of the revised MEQ30 in experimental sessions with psilocybin](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC5203697/)
- **PDF of the original paper**: [Barrett et al. 2015 (PDF)](https://files.csp.org/Psilocybin/Barrett2015MysticalExperience.pdf)
- **What it measures**: The quality and depth of the mystical-type experience across 4 dimensions
- **Why it matters**: Higher MEQ-30 scores during the experience are one of the strongest predictors of beneficial outcomes weeks and months later, for both distressed and healthy individuals
- **Scoring**: 6-point Likert scale (0 = "none; not at all" → 5 = "extreme, more than any other time in my life")
- **"Complete Mystical Experience"**: Scoring ≥60% on all four subscales (Barrett et al., 2015)

**Factor 1 — Mystical (15 items)**:
1. Freedom from the limitations of your personal self and feeling a unity or bond with what was felt to be greater than your personal self
2. Experience of pure being and pure awareness (beyond the world of sense impressions)
3. Experience of oneness in relation to an "inner world" within
4. Experience of the fusion of your personal self into a larger whole
5. Experience of unity with ultimate reality
6. Feeling that you experienced eternity or infinity
7. Experience of oneness or unity with objects and/or persons perceived in your surroundings
8. Experience of the insight that "all is One"
9. Awareness of the life or living presence in all things
10. Gain of insightful knowledge experienced at an intuitive level
11. Certainty of encounter with ultimate reality (in the sense of being able to "know" and "see" what is really real at some point during your experience)
12. You are convinced now, as you look back on your experience, that in it you encountered ultimate reality (i.e., that you "knew" and "saw" what was really real)
13. Sense of being at a spiritual height
14. Sense of reverence
15. Feeling that you experienced something profoundly sacred and holy

**Factor 2 — Positive Mood (6 items)**:
16. Experience of amazement
17. Feelings of tenderness and gentleness
18. Feelings of peace and tranquility
19. Experience of ecstasy
20. Sense of awe or awesomeness
21. Feelings of joy

**Factor 3 — Transcendence of Time and Space (6 items)**:
22. Loss of your usual sense of time
23. Loss of your usual sense of space
24. Loss of usual awareness of where you were
25. Sense of being "outside of" time, beyond past and future
26. Being in a realm with no space boundaries
27. Experience of timelessness

**Factor 4 — Ineffability (3 items)**:
28. Sense that the experience cannot be described adequately in words
29. Feeling that you could not do justice to your experience by describing it in words
30. Feeling that it would be difficult to communicate your own experience to others who have not had similar experiences

### 2. EDI — Ego Dissolution Inventory (8 items)
- **Source**: Imperial College London (Nour, Evans, Nutt & Carhart-Harris, 2016)
- **Paper**: [Ego-Dissolution and Psychedelics: Validation of the EDI](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4906025/)
- **Full questionnaire PDF**: [EDI Questionnaire (PDF)](https://matthewnour.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/01/EDI.pdf)
- **What it measures**: The dissolution of the normal sense of self — a hallmark of the psychedelic experience
- **Why it matters**: Ego dissolution correlates strongly with mystical experience (rho = 0.735) and is specific to psychedelics (not cocaine, not alcohol). It's dose-dependent with psychedelic drugs.
- **Scoring**: Visual Analogue Scale 0–100 (0 = "No, not more than usually" → 100 = "Yes, entirely or completely"). Total EDI score = mean of all 8 items.
- **Internal consistency**: Cronbach's alpha = 0.93 (excellent)

**Items**:
1. I experienced a dissolution of my "self" or ego
2. I felt at one with the universe
3. I felt a sense of union with others
4. I experienced a decrease in my sense of self-importance
5. I experienced a disintegration of my "self" or ego
6. I felt far less absorbed by my own issues and concerns
7. I lost all sense of ego
8. All notion of self and identity dissolved away

---

### 3. EBI — Emotional Breakthrough Inventory (6 items)
- **Source**: Imperial College London (Roseman, Haijen, Idialu-Ikato, Kaelen, Watts & Carhart-Harris, 2019)
- **Paper**: [Emotional breakthrough and psychedelics: Validation of the EBI](https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/0269881119855974)
- **Full PDF**: [EBI Paper (PDF)](https://unlimitedsciences.org/wp-content/uploads/2023/11/EmotionalBreakthroughsandPsychedelics-Rosemanet.al_.pdf)
- **What it measures**: Cathartic emotional release — facing and overcoming difficult emotions/memories during the experience
- **Why it matters**: EBI is a **distinct** component from mystical experience. EBI + MEQ + CEQ combined predict changes in wellbeing (r=0.45, p=0.0005). EBI scores are higher when the psychedelic is taken with therapeutic planning and intent. Emotional breakthrough may actually be a stronger predictor of depression improvement than mystical experience in clinical settings.
- **Scoring**: Visual Analogue Scale 0–100. Total = sum of all 6 items (range 0–600).
- **Internal consistency**: Cronbach's alpha = 0.932

**Items** (the 6 validated items from the factor analysis):
1. I experienced a resolution of a personal conflict or trauma
2. I felt able to explore challenging emotions and memories
3. I had an emotional breakthrough
4. I was able to get a sense of closure on an emotional problem
5. I achieved an emotional release followed by a sense of relief
6. I faced emotionally difficult feelings that I usually push aside

---

### 4. SWEMWBS — Short Warwick-Edinburgh Mental Wellbeing Scale (7 items)
- **Source**: University of Warwick & University of Edinburgh (Tennant et al., 2007; Stewart-Brown et al., 2009)
- **Paper**: [SWEMWBS performance in clinical sample](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8611866/)
- **What it measures**: Positive mental wellbeing — both feeling good and functioning well
- **Why it matters**: This is the **baseline and delta measure**. Used in psychedelic research (including the EBI validation study) as the primary dependent variable. By taking this before AND after (at 2 weeks, 1 month, 3 months), users can see concrete shifts in their overall mental wellbeing.
- **Scoring**: 5-point Likert (1 = "None of the time" → 5 = "All of the time"). Raw sum range: 7–35. Higher = better wellbeing.
- **Population norm**: Mean ~23.6 (UK adult population). Score ≤19.3 = low wellbeing (1 SD below mean).
- **Used as primary outcome measure in**: Roseman et al. 2019 (EBI study), Haijen et al. 2018, and numerous psychedelic trial follow-ups.

**Items** (each rated over the past 2 weeks):
1. I've been feeling optimistic about the future
2. I've been feeling useful
3. I've been feeling relaxed
4. I've been dealing with problems well
5. I've been thinking clearly
6. I've been feeling close to other people
7. I've been able to make up my own mind about things

---

### 5. Psychedelic Integration Scales (Frymann et al., 2022)
- **Source**: (Frymann, Moreno, Guss & Summers, 2022)
- **Paper**: [The Psychedelic Integration Scales](https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/psychology/articles/10.3389/fpsyg.2022.863247/full)
- **What they measure**: Two separate scales — (a) Engagement in integration behaviors and (b) Experienced integration (how settled the experience feels)
- **Why they matter**: The experience itself is only part of the story. Integration — actively making sense of and applying insights — is what turns a single experience into lasting change.

**Engaged Integration Scale (selected items for our use)**:
- I've given myself mental space to reconnect to the experience
- I've read, viewed, or listened to informative content relevant to my experience
- I've gained insight into my experience through talking with supportive people
- I've spent time in silent contemplation of my experience
- I've spent time in nature to nurture my experience
- I've followed up on my experience with focused attention practice (meditation, mindfulness, journaling, visualization, etc.)
- I've applied the learnings from my experience to my life
- I've found ways to carry the intentions I had for my experience into my daily life

**Experienced Integration Scale (selected items)**:
- I feel at peace with my experience
- I feel more balanced since my experience
- I have a continued sense of open-minded curiosity about my experience
- I feel a sense of harmony between my inner experience and my outer life

---

### 6. Psychedelic Preparedness Scale — PPS (Haijen et al., 2024)
- **Source**: (Haijen et al., 2024)
- **Paper**: [Development and validation of the PPS](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41598-024-53829-z)
- **What it measures**: How well-prepared someone is for their upcoming psychedelic experience, across 4 factors
- **Why it matters**: Preparation level significantly predicts the quality of the acute experience (higher MEQ, higher EBI, lower challenging experiences) and subsequent wellbeing outcomes. Users in the "High Preparation" group had significantly better outcomes across the board.
- **4 Factors**: Knowledge-Expectations, Intention-Preparation, Psychophysical-Readiness, Support-Planning

We adapt the spirit of this scale into our pre-experience intention-setting prompts rather than using it as a formal scored instrument.

---

### 7. Additional References

- **Challenging Experience Questionnaire (CEQ)** — Barrett et al., 2016. [Paper](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC5551678/). 26 items measuring difficult aspects (grief, fear, paranoia, isolation, etc.). We include a simplified version as an optional section.
- **MEQ-4 (Brief Mystical Experience Questionnaire)** — Strickland, Garcia-Romeu & Johnson, 2024. [Paper](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11658653/). A 4-item brief version for quick assessment. Could be used for check-ins.
- **Psychological Insight Questionnaire (PIQ)** — Davis et al., 2021. Measures insight gained during the experience.
- **MAPS Integration Workbook** — [PDF](https://spectrumpsychwa.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/04/MAPS-Integration-Workbook.pdf). Excellent framework for the integration journaling prompts.
- **Intention-Setting Research** — Marrocu et al., 2024. [Paper](https://scholarsarchive.library.albany.edu/cgi/viewcontent.cgi?article=1152&context=etd). Intentions that are specific, relevant, flexible, and integrated after the experience predict improvements in wellbeing.

## App Architecture

### Three-Phase Journal Design

The app is structured as a **three-phase personal journal** that mirrors the actual arc used in clinical psychedelic research: Preparation → Experience → Integration.

Each phase is a separate entry tied to a specific "journey" (a single psychedelic experience). Users can have multiple journeys. The key UX insight: **the value is in the comparison view** — being able to look at Phase 1 and Phase 3 side by side and see how your relationship to your own mind has shifted.

---

### Phase 1: Before — "Setting the Compass" (Pre-experience)

**When**: Days or hours before the experience
**Purpose**: Establish a baseline emotional/mental state AND set intentions

**Contains**:

**A) Baseline Wellbeing Snapshot (SWEMWBS — 7 items)**
- Full SWEMWBS as described above
- 5-point Likert scale
- This is the "before" photo that gets compared to "after"

**B) Current Inner Landscape (custom reflective questions)**
- "What is your current relationship with yourself?" (free text)
- "What emotions have been most present for you lately?" (free text)
- "What are you most afraid of right now?" (free text)
- "What are you most grateful for right now?" (free text)
- Rate on 0–10 slider: "How connected do you feel to the people in your life?"
- Rate on 0–10 slider: "How much clarity do you feel about your life direction?"
- Rate on 0–10 slider: "How at peace do you feel with yourself?"

**C) Intention Setting (adapted from PPS and integration research)**
- "What is your primary intention for this experience?" (free text — encourage specificity)
- "What would you like to explore or understand better about yourself?" (free text)
- "What are you willing to let go of?" (free text)
- "Is there anything you're afraid might come up? If so, how do you want to meet it?" (free text)
- "What does a 'successful' experience look like for you — and can you hold that loosely?" (free text)

**D) Practical Context (metadata)**
- Date
- Substance (dropdown: Psilocybin, LSD, DMT, Ayahuasca, Mescaline, MDMA, Ketamine, Other)
- Approximate dose (free text)
- Setting description (free text, e.g., "at home with a friend", "retreat center", etc.)
- Who is with you / who is your sitter? (free text)

---

### Phase 2: After the Experience — "Mapping the Territory" (24–72 hours post)

**When**: Ideally within 24–48 hours. The neuroplasticity window is open for 2–4 days post-experience (Grieco et al., 2022). This is the critical window for reflection.
**Purpose**: Capture the quality of the experience while it's fresh, and give the user language for what happened

**Contains**:

**A) Raw Impressions (free text, first)**
- "Before we ask specific questions, take a moment to write freely about your experience. What happened? What did you feel? What surprised you?" (large text area)
- "If you could capture the essence of the experience in a single image or metaphor, what would it be?" (free text)

**B) Mystical Experience — MEQ-30 (30 items)**
- Full MEQ-30 as described above
- 6-point scale: 0–5
- Display results grouped by factor with subscale scores
- Show whether they met the threshold for a "complete mystical experience" (≥60% on all 4 subscales)

**C) Ego Dissolution — EDI (8 items)**
- Full EDI as described above
- Slider 0–100 (VAS)
- Display mean score

**D) Emotional Breakthrough — EBI (6 items)**
- Full EBI as described above
- Slider 0–100 (VAS)
- Display total score (0–600)

**E) Challenging Aspects (simplified, optional)**
- "Did you experience anything frightening or difficult?" (yes/no → if yes, expand)
- "Rate the overall difficulty of the experience" (slider 0–10)
- "What was the most challenging moment?" (free text)
- "How did you relate to the difficulty — did you resist, surrender, something else?" (free text)
- "Looking back, do you feel the challenging aspects were valuable?" (5-point: Not at all → Extremely)

**F) Intention Revisited**
- Display the user's original intentions from Phase 1 (read-only)
- "How did the experience relate to your original intentions?" (free text)
- "Did anything completely unexpected emerge?" (free text)

---

### Phase 3: Integration — "Walking the Path" (2 weeks, 1 month, 3 months)

**When**: Multiple check-ins over time. Suggested at 2 weeks, 1 month, and 3 months. Users can add more.
**Purpose**: Track how the experience is settling into daily life and compare against baseline

**Contains**:

**A) Wellbeing Re-assessment (SWEMWBS — same 7 items as Phase 1)**
- Identical SWEMWBS from Phase 1
- App calculates and displays the delta (change scores)
- Visual comparison: before vs. now

**B) Inner Landscape Re-assessment (same custom questions from Phase 1)**
- Same sliders: connectedness, clarity, inner peace (0–10)
- Same free text questions about relationship with self, prevalent emotions
- App displays Phase 1 answers alongside for comparison

**C) Integration Behaviors (adapted from Psychedelic Integration Scales)**
- 8 items from the Engaged Integration Scale
- 5-point scale: "Not at all" → "Very much"
- These help the user reflect on whether they're actively doing the work of integration

**D) Experienced Integration (adapted from Psychedelic Integration Scales)**
- 4 items from the Experienced Integration Scale
- 5-point scale
- Measures how settled and integrated the experience feels

**E) Intention Integration**
- Display original intentions from Phase 1
- "Have you found ways to carry your intentions into daily life?" (free text)
- "What concrete changes, if any, have you made since the experience?" (free text)
- "What insights from the experience feel most alive right now?" (free text)
- "What, if anything, has faded that you wish hadn't?" (free text)

**F) Open Reflection**
- "If you could write a letter to yourself before the experience, what would you say?" (free text)
- "What do you understand now that you didn't before?" (free text)

## Comparison View — The Core Value Prop

The most important feature of the app. A dedicated view that shows:

1. **SWEMWBS Before vs. After**: Side-by-side or overlay chart showing each of the 7 items, with the raw score change and direction. This makes the wellbeing shift tangible.

2. **Inner Landscape Sliders Before vs. After**: Visual comparison of the 0–10 ratings (connectedness, clarity, inner peace) at each time point.

3. **Intention Arc**: Shows the original intention, how the experience related to it, and how it's been integrated — as a narrative thread.

4. **Experience Profile**: A radar/spider chart or similar visualization of the Phase 2 scores:
   - MEQ-30 subscale scores (4 axes: Mystical, Positive Mood, Time-Space, Ineffability)
   - EDI mean score
   - EBI total score
   - Challenge level
   This gives a "fingerprint" of the experience.

5. **Integration Timeline**: If multiple Phase 3 entries exist, show the SWEMWBS and slider scores over time as a line chart, so the user can see the trajectory.

## Technical Requirements

### Stack
- **Frontend**: React (single-page app) or Next.js
- **Storage**: All data stored **locally** (localStorage or IndexedDB). No server, no accounts, no cloud. Privacy is non-negotiable — this is deeply personal data.
- **Export**: Option to export all data as JSON (for backup) and individual journeys as PDF
- **No authentication needed** — single-user, local-first

### Key UX Principles

1. **Warm, not clinical.** The language should feel like a thoughtful friend asking good questions, not a doctor with a clipboard. The questionnaire items from MEQ-30/EDI/EBI are inherently a bit clinical — wrap them in context that explains what they're measuring and why, without jargon.

2. **Progressive disclosure.** Don't show all 30 MEQ items on one scroll. Group by factor, explain what each factor measures in plain language, let users expand/collapse sections.

3. **No pressure to complete everything.** Every section should be optional. Some people will want to fill out every item; others will just want the free-text journaling. Both are valid.

4. **The comparison view is the payoff.** Make it beautiful and clear. This is the moment where someone goes "oh, I've actually changed."

5. **Dark mode default.** People often journal at night or in introspective states. Soft, warm colors. No harsh whites.

6. **Mobile-first.** People will journal from their phone, especially in the 24-48 hours after an experience.

7. **Explain the science lightly.** Each instrument section should have a small expandable "Why this question?" that briefly explains the research behind it (e.g., "This is from the Mystical Experience Questionnaire developed at Johns Hopkins. Higher scores here have been shown to predict lasting positive changes in wellbeing and life satisfaction.")

## Scoring Reference

### MEQ-30 Scoring
- Each item: 0–5 (6-point scale)
- **Subscale scores**: Mean of items in each factor
- **Total score**: Mean of all 30 items (range 0–5) or sum (range 0–150)
- **Complete Mystical Experience**: Score ≥60% of maximum on ALL four subscales
  - Mystical (15 items): mean ≥ 3.0
  - Positive Mood (6 items): mean ≥ 3.0
  - Transcendence of Time/Space (6 items): mean ≥ 3.0
  - Ineffability (3 items): mean ≥ 3.0

### EDI Scoring
- Each item: 0–100 (VAS)
- **Total EDI score**: Mean of all 8 items (range 0–100)

### EBI Scoring
- Each item: 0–100 (VAS)
- **Total EBI score**: Sum of all 6 items (range 0–600)
- Note: In some studies this is also reported as a mean (0–100)

### SWEMWBS Scoring
- Each item: 1–5
- **Raw total**: Sum of 7 items (range 7–35)
- Higher = better wellbeing
- **Meaningful change**: A change of ≥3 points on the raw score is considered clinically meaningful
- **Population reference**: UK adult mean ≈ 23.6, low wellbeing cut-point ≤ 19.3
